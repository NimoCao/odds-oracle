# S02 Value Dog And Under

## Positioning

Use this strategy to exploit overreaction to favorites: strong-team heat, undervalued underdogs, and low-score scripts where the favorite is unlikely to win by margin.

## Suitable Markets

- Underdog受让.
- Draw or double-chance style positions when available.
- 小球 or split-line unders.
- Favorite win but not covering, only when the market supports that expression.

## Entry Signals

Look for several of these:

- Favorite is popular but has rotation, travel, low urgency, poor chance quality, or weak finishing form.
- Underdog has compact defense, transition outlet, set-piece threat, or keeper/center-back profile that can absorb pressure.
- Match incentives support risk control rather than open tempo.
- Total line is inflated by team names or recent high-scoring noise.
- Handicap line asks the favorite to win by too much.

## Staking

- Default stake: 2%-4%.
- Strong spot: 5%-6%.
- Same-match cap: 7%.
- If combining underdog and under in the same match, treat them as correlated and reduce total stake.

## Forbidden Conditions

- Avoid when the underdog has multiple defensive absences.
- Avoid when the favorite lineup is full strength and stylistically attacks the underdog's clearest weakness.
- Avoid under positions after late lineup news adds attacking quality or removes defensive structure.
- Avoid if odds have collapsed below the value threshold.

## Output Bias

Explain why the favorite may win narrowly, draw, or fail to cover. Do not frame the pick as simply "weak team will not lose"; tie it to price and handicap.
